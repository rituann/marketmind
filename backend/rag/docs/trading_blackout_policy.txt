Internal Trading Blackout Policy
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Issuing Authority: Chief Compliance Officer
Review Cycle: Annual

PURPOSE
This policy establishes mandatory blackout periods during which employees are prohibited from trading in company securities or securities of designated restricted companies. The policy is designed to prevent insider trading violations and protect both employees and the firm from legal liability.

SCOPE
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, directors, and officers of the firm, including all subsidiaries. It covers trading in equities, options, convertible bonds, ETFs concentrated in restricted names, and derivatives referenced to restricted securities.

QUARTERLY EARNINGS BLACKOUT
All employees are prohibited from trading in firm securities during the period beginning 14 calendar days before the end of each fiscal quarter and ending 48 hours after the public release of quarterly earnings results.

Fiscal quarter end dates: March 31, June 30, September 30, December 31.

DEAL-SPECIFIC BLACKOUT — RESTRICTED LIST
When the firm is engaged in a material transaction (M&A advisory, underwriting, due diligence, restructuring advisory), the relevant securities are placed on the Restricted List. Trading in Restricted List securities requires advance approval from the CCO and is presumptively denied absent exceptional circumstances.

PERSONAL TRADING REQUIREMENTS
Pre-Clearance: All employees in Investment Banking, Research, and Trading must obtain pre-clearance for any personal securities transaction above $5,000 in value. Pre-clearance requests must be submitted at least one business day in advance.

Holding Period: Securities acquired through pre-cleared transactions must be held for a minimum of 30 calendar days before sale.

Reporting: All employees must report new personal securities accounts within 10 days of opening and submit annual holdings certifications by January 31 each year.

SHORT-TERM TRADING PROHIBITION
Employees are prohibited from profiting from the purchase and sale, or sale and purchase, of firm securities within any period of less than six months (Section 16(b) of the Securities Exchange Act).

CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION
Violations of this policy may result in:
- Mandatory disgorgement of profits
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Referral to regulatory authorities (SEC, FINRA, FCA)
- Personal civil and criminal liability under securities laws

EXCEPTIONS
Exceptions to this policy may be granted by the CCO in extraordinary circumstances. All exception requests must be in writing and retained in the compliance records for a minimum of five years. Exceptions are not available for Restricted List securities.
